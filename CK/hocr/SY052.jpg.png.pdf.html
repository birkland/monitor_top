Each point on the dial represents a different cabinet air temperature which will automatically be maintained by the control. he warmest position is No. 1 a coldest No. 12. As pointed out above, position No. 6 should give the best all-around operation, but if colder or warmer cabinet air temperatures are desired, they can be obtained by selecting the proper number on the dial. However, with the control dial set at the warmer positions, a slight defrosting of the SUPER FREEZER may occur. This is perfectly normal and in no way affects the operation or elneiency or’ the refrigerator. This de— frosting can be eliminated by setting the control dial to a higher number.

The STEADY-COLD CONTROL will better pro— tect food from freezing in cool room temperatures. The SUPER FREEZER temperature limits are automatically raised as the room gets cooler. Some defrosting or the SUPER FREEZER will naturally occur under such conditions, even with the dial at position No. 6.

Complete defrosting of the SUPER FREEZER is obtained by setting the dial to the “Defrost" position, After defrosting is complete, the dial should be returned to the normal position No. 6. or whatever position has been found to be the most satisfactory.

The armature is made of a rather heavy copper section attached to a spring steel leaf from the springing point. The end of the armature circles the upper part of the middle leg on which the coil is placed, and forms a single shorbcircuited turn above the coil.

Current passing through the coil induces current in the short-circuited turn, the action of which tends to repel the armature. If the current is greater than the pick—up current, the force is suﬁiciem to move the armature outward to close the starting contacts. When the current decreases to the drop» out value, the spring tension of the armature overcomes the magnetic force and opens the contacts.

Caution: Never attempt to adjust, repair or replace an armature or a contact.

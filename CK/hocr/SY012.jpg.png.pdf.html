With the %hp, 60-cycle motors used before 1939, there is a capacitor in series with the starting winding. Its purpose is to increase the starting torque and reduce the starting current. At the beginning of 1939, this motor was redesigned to eliminate the capacitor.

The 1/ 10-hp, 25-cycle motor used with “two“size compressors does not have a capacitor in series with the starting winding; but the %-hp, 25~cycle motor on “three" size compressors does.

The special motor in CFS-1 machines has two capacitors in the starting winding circuit. This starting winding is in the circuit when the machine is running and one capacitor is in series with it. The other capacitor is put in parallel with the first one during the momentary starting period.

The motor in certain CK machines, designed to be run from direct current service through a rotary converter, has two independent phases.

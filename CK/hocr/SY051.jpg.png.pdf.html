1940 Illuminated Controls (M1A203, MIAZW)

The control is mounted to the lamp housing by two screws in the same way that standard controls are mounted to the escutcheon plate. The lamp housing attaches to the evaporator like a standard escutcheon plate.

The illuminated escutcheon plate contains an insert or polystyrene which has the property of diffusing the light thrown on it from the larnp in back of it. The outer knob is of the same ma- terial. The escutcheon plate is held to the lamp housing by two ears on the top of the housing and a snap joint at the bottom.

The shaft of the outer knob has a Hat on the end which registers with a crimped spring in the recess in the control knob.

The lamp is located in back of the escutcheon plate and fits in a molded rubber socket in the enameled top plate. It is a (3-15 Mazda, 7 watt, clear, ll0/l25 volts.

The M1A203 control has standard temperature limits and is used on machines charged with sul- phur dioxide (S05). The MIAZO4 control has special temperature limits and is used on machines charged with Freon-12.

The average refrigerated shelf temperature of an 50, machine is about 4° or 5° F. warmer than the evaporator bottom. The average refrigerated shelf temperature or a Freon-12 machine is ap- proximately equal to that of the evaporator bot- tom. Consequently, the control limits are several degrees warmer than those of an S0: machine.

The bellows tube bulb is attached above the middle of the left evaporator header. All other bellows tube bulbs are in contact with about the middle of the first or second channel on the left side of stainless steel evaporators.

The location of the bellows tube bulb above the center of the evaporator header was selected to give practically the same cabinet air tempera- ture in all room temperatures between 60° and 100° F. The bulb is above the refrigerant level in the evaporator so its temperature is affected by the heat leakage into the top of the refrigerator as well as by the refrigerant temperature. The higher the room temperature, the greater the heat leakage and the lower the evaporator temperature is made in order to keep the same cabinet air temperature.
